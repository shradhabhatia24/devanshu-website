Why This Book

Starting with foundational concepts from physics, the book builds a bridge between analytical thinking and ancient wisdom — so your rational mind can actually believe what it's reading.

A Clear Framework, Not Noise

A simple three-step meditation framework: Preparation → Turning Inward → Focused Awareness, provides a structured approach to building a meditation ritual which you can follow everyday.

Written for Modern Professionals

Devanshu came to meditation not as a monk but as a strategy consultant under pressure. He writes for people navigating demanding careers who quietly sense there must be a calmer way to live.

A Fellow Seeker, Not a Guru

Devanshu is refreshingly honest: he's not enlightened and doesn't claim to be. He shares his own personal meditation journey, his learnings, information about his teachers — and this honesty is the book's greatest strength.

Tangible, Everyday Benefits

Inside the Book

Four questions.
One clear path forward.

The book is structured around the four questions Devanshu was trying to answer when he first set out to understand meditation. Each section builds on the last, taking you from why to what and all the way to the how.

01

Chapter One

Why Should I Meditate?

Before you can commit to a practice, you need to understand what you're actually doing when you close your eyes. This chapter demystifies the ultimate goal of meditation. Starting with a gentle introduction to quantum physics, it then explores the human body through a spiritual lens, and finally arrives at the deepest purpose of meditation: recognising your true nature as pure awareness.

02

Chapter Two

What Benefits Will Meditation Bring Me?

Once you understand the why, the rational mind wants proof. This chapter explores meditation's benefits through two lenses: the transformational everyday impact and the deeper spiritual perspective. The everyday benefits alone, Devanshu argues, are powerful enough to justify the practice. The spiritual dimension is the gift that comes with time.

03

Chapter Three

Where Does Yoga Fit into All of This?

"Yoga" has become synonymous with physical exercise, but its deeper essence is almost entirely about the path to meditation. This chapter attempts to demystify yoga and while doing so, it leans on ancient wisdom to develop a systematic three-step framework which can be used to build a daily meditation ritual.

04

Chapter Four

How Should I Meditate?

With the why, what, and context in place, this chapter teaches you the how. Leaning on his experience, Devanshu presents techniques and practices in each step of the three-step meditation framework. He also tries to provide the meaning behind the different practices to enable the rational mind to believe in it. The chapter also covers how long to meditate, the best time of day, and how to build a sustainable daily practice.

Devanshu Dalmia

Commercial & Strategy Professional · First-Time Author · Meditation Practitioner

I'm a commercial and strategy professional with over a decade of experience in high-pressure corporate environments. I hold an MBA from London Business School and an engineering degree from Nanyang Technological University in Singapore. Based in Amsterdam, I've lived and worked in seven countries.

My journey with meditation began in 2020 — during a period when I couldn't sleep, my body was falling apart, and I was navigating a struggling family business at the height of COVID-19. I was twenty-eight but felt sixty. A performance coach led me through a guided meditation that brought a deep sense of calm, and that got me excited about this space.

From there I started reading, attending retreats, visiting ashrams. Slowly, something began to shift. Today, meditation is my daily anchor — the thing that helps me stay clear-headed, focused, and grounded amid the demands of a fast-moving corporate career.

I wrote this book because I needed it and couldn't find it. It's the beginner's guide I wish had existed when I first asked, "I want to meditate, but where do I begin?"
