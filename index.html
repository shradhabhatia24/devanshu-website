Inside the Book

Four questions.
One clear path forward.

The book is structured around the four questions Devanshu was trying to answer when he first set out to understand meditation — each section builds on the last, taking you from "why?" all the way to "how?"

01

Chapter One

Why Should I Meditate?

Before you can commit to a practice, you need to understand what you're actually doing when you close your eyes. This chapter demystifies the ultimate goal of meditation. Starting with a gentle introduction to quantum physics — wave–particle duality and the observer effect — it explores the human body through a spiritual lens via the five energy sheaths (Koshas), and finally arrives at the deepest purpose of meditation: recognising your true nature as pure awareness.

Quantum Physics The Observer Effect The Five Koshas Consciousness Your True Self
02

Chapter Two

What Benefits Will Meditation Bring Me?

Once you understand the why, the rational mind wants proof. This chapter explores meditation's benefits through two lenses: the transformational everyday impact — greater joy, sharper focus, cultivating fearlessness, and the ability to manifest — and the deeper spiritual perspective. The everyday benefits alone, Devanshu argues, are powerful enough to justify the practice. The spiritual dimension is the gift that comes with time.

Joy & Wellbeing Focus & Clarity Fearlessness Manifestation Spiritual Growth
03

Chapter Three

Where Does Yoga Fit into All of This?

"Yoga" has become synonymous with physical exercise — but its deeper essence is almost entirely about the path to meditation. This chapter reclaims that original meaning, explaining yoga's eight-limbed path (Ashtanga Yoga) and showing how the chakras, pranayama, and energy channels (nadis) are practical tools for preparing the mind and body to meditate effectively. It decodes the jargon so the "how" chapter actually makes sense.

Ashtanga Yoga Chakras Pranayama Nadis Yogic Philosophy
04

Chapter Four

How Should I Meditate?

With the why, what, and context in place, this chapter teaches you the how. Devanshu presents a practical three-step meditation framework: (1) Preparation — postures and breathwork; (2) Turning Inward — body scan, gratitude, and the Hamsa breath; and (3) Focused Awareness — resting attention on breath, mantra, energy centres, or sound until the mind becomes still. The chapter also covers how long to meditate, the best time of day, and how to build a sustainable daily practice.

3-Step Framework Breathwork Body Scan Mantra Focused Awareness Daily Practice

Commercial & Strategy Professional · First-Time Author · Meditation Practitioner

I'm a commercial and strategy professional with over a decade of experience in high-pressure corporate environments. I hold an MBA from London Business School and an engineering degree from Nanyang Technological University in Singapore. Based in Amsterdam, I've lived and worked in seven countries.

My journey with meditation began in 2020 — during a period when I couldn't sleep, my body was falling apart, and I was navigating a struggling family business at the height of COVID-19. I was twenty-eight but felt sixty. A performance coach led me through a guided meditation that brought a deep sense of calm, and that single session changed the course of my life.

From there I started reading, attending retreats, visiting ashrams. Slowly, something began to shift. Today, meditation is my daily anchor — the thing that helps me stay clear-headed, focused, and grounded amid the demands of a fast-moving corporate career.

I wrote this book because I needed it and couldn't find it. It's the beginner's guide I wish had existed when I first asked, "I want to meditate — but where do I begin?"
